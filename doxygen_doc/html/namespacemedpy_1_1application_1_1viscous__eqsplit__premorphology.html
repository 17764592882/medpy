00001 """
00002                   Takes a gradient image as input and performs on various levels of its
00003                   topographic representation morphological closing operations as required
00004                   by the viscous watershed. This step is a pre-processing step that has
00005                   to be followed by a standard watershed segmentation, but makes it
00006                   behave as if the a viscous liquid was used.
00007                   To separate the gradient image into different regions, its topography
00008                   is considered and the morphological closing in applied to vertical
00009                   slices of said topographical representation.
00010                   To take into account the different range of intensity values (and
00011                   therefore different heights of the topography) that can appear,
00012                   a histogram of the images intensity values is created and subsequently
00013                   equalized. This way the vertical slices are of different size but
00014                   contain nearly exactely the same amount of pixels.
00015                   This step becomes especially important when the input image contains
00016                   very steep gradients, as may for example appear if a metal implant
00017                   is inside the scan range (metal being of a high density and leading
00018                   to very bright spots).
00019                   The provided 'sections' parameter determines the number of bin of the
00020                   equalized histogram and therefore the number of slices in which the
00021                   topography is split.
00022                   The 'dsize' parameter determines the sizxe of the biggest disc applied
00023                   in the closing operation. In each subsequent slice a smaller disc is
00024                   applied.
00025                   The resulting image will be saved under the same name and type as the
00026                   input image, only with an '_viscous' and its parameters as suffix.
00027                   See "The viscous watershed transform" by Vachier, Corinne and Meyer,
00028                   Fernand (Journal of Mathematical Imaging and Vision) for more details.
